# Product Overview

The AI-Powered Geospatial Reconstruction Platform is an intelligent satellite imagery restoration system designed to reconstruct cloud-covered regions in LISS-IV satellite imagery using geospatial intelligence, historical context, and artificial intelligence.

The platform transforms incomplete satellite observations into analysis-ready geospatial products that can be used for decision-making, monitoring, planning, and scientific analysis.

The system is designed as an operational workflow rather than a standalone AI model.

---

# Problem Statement

Optical satellite imagery frequently suffers from:

* Thick cloud cover
* Thin cloud cover
* Cirrus clouds
* Cloud shadows
* Atmospheric disturbances

These obstructions hide critical information present on the Earth's surface.

As a result:

* Agricultural monitoring becomes difficult
* Disaster assessment becomes delayed
* Urban planning loses accuracy
* Change detection becomes unreliable
* Geospatial analysis quality decreases

---

# Existing Challenges

Traditional approaches often rely on:

* Manual inspection
* Cloud masking
* Data discarding
* Simple interpolation

These methods do not reconstruct the hidden information beneath cloud-covered regions.

Many existing solutions also fail to provide:

* Explainability
* Confidence estimation
* Historical context integration
* Operational workflows

---

# Proposed Solution

The platform introduces a multi-layer intelligent reconstruction workflow.

Instead of merely removing clouds, the platform:

1. Understands the uploaded dataset.
2. Extracts geospatial intelligence.
3. Retrieves temporal context.
4. Detects cloud-covered regions.
5. Reconstructs hidden terrain.
6. Estimates confidence.
7. Produces analysis-ready outputs.
